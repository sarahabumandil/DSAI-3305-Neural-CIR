# Presentation: How Far Has Conversational Information Retrieval Evolved?

## 1. The Concept
- **Definition:** CIR is the next generation of search where users interact with information systems through natural, multi-turn dialogue.
- **Shift:** Moving from "Keyword-based" search to "Intent-based" conversations.

## 2. How It Works
- **Neural Retrieval:** Using Dense Vectors (Embeddings) to understand meaning instead of matching words.
- **Context Management:** Tracking the history of the conversation to resolve pronouns (e.g., "Tell me more about *him*").
- **LLM Integration:** Using Large Language Models to generate human-like responses based on retrieved data.

## 3. Where It Is Used
- **AI Search Engines:** Like Perplexity AI, Bing Chat, and Google Gemini.
- **Enterprise Knowledge:** Internal bots that help employees find documents via chat.
- **Customer Support:** Advanced bots that solve complex issues without human intervention.

## 4. Latest Developments
- **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation):** The ability of the AI to read a specific document and answer questions based *only* on it.
- **Multimodal CIR:** Searching using a mix of text, images, and voice in the same conversation.

## 5. Current Challenges
- **Hallucination:** The system providing confident but incorrect information.
- **Context Window:** Difficulty in remembering very long conversations.
- **Evaluation:** It is harder to measure if a "conversation" is successful compared to just a "link click."
